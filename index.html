# Spring Buildup ![](img/snow.jpg) ### Matthew Richardson - 1st March 2021
## Spring Build Up? ![](img/flatpack.jpg) Notes: * Better late than never! * Get all your equipment made up now while it is quiet. * You can't catch a swarm in a flatpack box!
## Spring Cleaning ![](img/blowtorch.jpg) Notes: * Cleanliness is next to Godliness! * Clean equipment improves bee health. * Easier to work with clean gear - no sticky propolis etc. * Cleaning also allows you to spot any needed repairs.
## Spring to Life ![](img/population.jpg)![](img/winter-feeding.jpg) Notes: * Things which are Increasing in the hive: * Activity * Brood * Heat * Comb building * Things which are Decreasing: * Adult Bees * Stores * There is a cross-over point where die-off may outpace brood-rearing!
## Spring into Action! ![](img/scales.jpg)![](img/fondant.jpg)![](img/syrup.jpg) Notes: * Starvation always a late-season risk. * You must feed if low on stores * Little and often * Too much can cause chilling and colony can become honeybound * Stimulative feeding is a bit of a myth! * Syrup/fondant for energy and comb building * Pollen (rarely needed) for brood rearing * Excess brood-rearing risks stress and chilled brood if weather conditions worsen again.
## Spring the Lid ![](img/crownboard.jpg) Notes: * First inspections * Mid-March to start of April * Above 12C is ideal * Food and Egg-laying are key signs to look for * Count the seams of bees and check for healthy brood pattern. * Merge weak colonies * It is easier to split a strong colony later, than try to make 2 weak colonies strong
## What Springs to Mind... ![](img/bees.jpg)![](img/honey.jpg) Notes: * You can make Bees OR Honey - but not both! * Different approaches for each, but same starting points: * Strong, healthy colonies * Spare equipment on hand * A good schedule planned out
## Spring Honey ![](img/hives.jpg) Notes: * Strong colonies at the right time of year (6 weeks from egg to forager) * Plenty of spare supers to hand! * OSR * Rapid turnaround and extraction * Soft set (creaming)
## Full of the Joys of Spring ![](img/queen-emerge.jpg) Notes: * Splits, swarms and increase * Selection - choose your best stocks to increase from * Splits * 7-day inspections * Decide on method in advance (Pagden, Demaree, Snelgrove etc) * Queen-rearing * Can be as simple as swarm cells, or more complex e.g. grafting * Nucs * Split across multiple nucs for increase * Mating nucs for rapid increase (alongside queen-rearing)
